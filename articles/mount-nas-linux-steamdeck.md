<h2>Introduction</h2><p>If you have a NAS (Network Attached Storage), you can mount its shared folders directly to your Steam Deck or Linux PC. This allows you to access files, ROMs, or backups over your local network. We'll walk through the steps for both SMB (Windows-style) and NFS (Linux-style) shares.</p><h2>Requirements</h2><ul><li>Steam Deck or any Linux device</li><li>Access to a NAS with SMB or NFS enabled</li><li>NAS IP address and shared folder name</li></ul><h2>Option 1: Mount an SMB Share (Windows-style)</h2><h3>1. Install CIFS Utilities</h3><pre>sudo pacman -S cifs-utils</pre><h3>2. Create a Mount Folder</h3><pre>mkdir ~/NAS</pre><h3>3. Mount the Share</h3><p>Use this command, replacing with your values:</p><pre>sudo mount -t cifs //192.168.1.100/share ~/NAS -o username=youruser,password=yourpass,uid=1000,gid=1000</pre><p>To avoid typing the password every time, create a credentials file:</p><pre>echo "username=youruser" > ~/.nas-cred
echo "password=yourpass" >> ~/.nas-cred
chmod 600 ~/.nas-cred</pre><p>Then mount with:</p><pre>sudo mount -t cifs //192.168.1.100/share ~/NAS -o credentials=/home/deck/.nas-cred,uid=1000,gid=1000</pre><h2>Option 2: Mount an NFS Share (Linux-style)</h2><h3>1. Install NFS Tools</h3><pre>sudo pacman -S nfs-utils</pre><h3>2. Create a Mount Point</h3><pre>mkdir ~/NAS</pre><h3>3. Mount the Share</h3><pre>sudo mount -t nfs 192.168.1.100:/export/share ~/NAS</pre><h2>Tips & Tricks</h2><ul><li>Add the mount to <code>/etc/fstab</code> for auto-mounting on boot.</li><li>Make sure your NAS allows your device's IP or hostname access.</li><li>Use static IPs or hostnames for consistency.</li></ul><h2>Troubleshooting</h2><ul><li><strong>Permission denied:</strong> Check credentials or access rules on NAS.</li><li><strong>Share not found:</strong> Double-check IP and share path.</li><li><strong>Mount command hangs:</strong> Ensure NAS is powered on and reachable.</li></ul><h2>Conclusion</h2><p>Mounting a NAS share on your Steam Deck or Linux system gives you quick access to media, ROMs, backups, and more.
